How you can help!

1. Watch and Support 2D Animated Films

Choose 2D animated movies or shows on streaming platforms. The more views they get, the more studios will see there’s still demand for hand-drawn art.

2. Encourage Art and Drawing in Schools

Teachers can include drawing or basic animation projects in art or ICT classes, helping students understand storytelling through visuals.

3. Start 2D Animation Clubs

Schools can form clubs where students learn the basics of frame-by-frame animation, storytelling, and character design.

4. Share 2D Content on Social Media

Parents and students can share clips, fan art, or short animations online to celebrate 2D animation and raise awareness.

5. Invite Artists to Speak or Teach

Schools or communities can host talks or workshops with animators who work in 2D to inspire students and show career possibilities..

6. Support 2D Animators Online

Follow, like, and share independent 2D animators’ work on YouTube, Instagram, or Patreon. It helps them reach more people..

7. Make Posters and Spread Awareness

Create posters, digital art, or infographics that show why 2D animation matters and share them around school or on social media.

8. Celebrate Animation Day

Schools and families can organize small events, screenings, or art contests celebrating 2D animation.
