What is a Telegraph?

The telegraph is an early long-distance communication system that sends messages using electrical signals. It was the first technology that allowed information to move faster than a human or a horse.

The word “telegraph” means “writing at a distance.” Early versions used visible signals or mechanical arms, but the electric telegraph used wires and electricity to carry coded messages across great distances.

Telegraph keys like this one were used by operators to send coded messages.

By the mid-1800s, telegraphs connected cities, countries, and eventually continents with undersea cables. The system transformed business, journalism, and personal communication.

Telegraph receivers printed or clicked messages sent from faraway operators.
