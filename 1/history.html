History of the Telegraph

In the early 1800s, inventors around the world experimented with ways to send messages using electricity. Samuel Morse and Alfred Vail in the United States created a practical telegraph system in the 1830s, using dots and dashes known as Morse Code.

The Morse and Vail telegraph of 1837 — one of the first successful designs.

In 1844, the first public message was sent from Washington D.C. to Baltimore: “What hath God wrought.” Soon after, telegraph lines spread rapidly across America and Europe.

The first transcontinental telegraph in 1861 connected the East and West coasts of the United States.

By the 1860s, undersea cables connected continents, linking North America, Europe, Africa, and Asia. The world’s first transatlantic telegraph cable was completed in 1858.

A cross-section of the 1866 transatlantic telegraph cable, insulated with gutta-percha and wrapped in iron wire.
