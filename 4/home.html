Navigation

GPS, or Global Positioning System, is a satellite-based navigation system that allows users to determine their precise location anywhere on Earth. It is widely used in navigation, mapping, transportation, and many everyday applications like smartphones and cars.

Navigation is the art and science of determining the position and course of a ship, aircraft, or vehicle. Early humans navigated using landmarks, stars, and the Sun. Ancient civilizations like the Phoenicians, Greeks, and Polynesians developed methods for long-distance sea travel. The invention of the compass, maps, and later GPS revolutionized navigation, allowing accurate and global positioning for exploration, trade, and modern transportation.
