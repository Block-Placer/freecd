Stars

For thousands of years, sailors have looked to the stars to find their way across the open ocean. Stars act like natural landmarks in the night sky â€” their fixed positions make them perfect for guiding navigation. By identifying certain constellations, travelers could figure out which direction they were going long before compasses or GPS were invented.

One of the most important stars for navigation is the North Star, or Polaris. Because it stays nearly still above the North Pole, it helped sailors know which way was north. Navigators also used constellations like Orion, the Big Dipper, and the Southern Cross to estimate their position and travel routes. Even today, understanding how to use stars remains an important skill for astronomy and traditional navigation.
