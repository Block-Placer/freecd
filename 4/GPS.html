Information about GPS

Navigation is all about finding your way from one place to another. In the past, people used things like landmarks, the Sun, and stars to guide them. Civilizations such as the Phoenicians, Greeks, and Polynesians were experts at traveling long distances using these methods. Over time, inventions like the compass, maps, and eventually GPS changed the way we travel, making it faster, safer, and much more accurate.

The Global Positioning System, or GPS, is a network of satellites that helps people know exactly where they are on Earth. It works by using signals from at least four satellites to calculate your location. GPS was first built by the U.S. Department of Defense for military use, but today it’s used everywhere — in phones, cars, airplanes, and even farming. By measuring how long it takes signals to reach your device, GPS can find your position with amazing accuracy — often within just a few meters.
