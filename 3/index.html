If you have a smartphone, you may be familiar with the common problems that come with it. Smartphones are supposed to make life easier as there are literally millions of apps available for them. It is easy to rely on smartphones for almost everything, from checking out bank balance to reading the news. But what happens when your phone starts to slow down or stops working altogether?

The underappreciation

The under-appreciation of smartphones as disposable items, rather than powerful tools, raises significant problems across a number of contexts. Environmentally, the drive to create new models and rapid upgrades, along with built-in obsolescence, and consumer habits creates immense e-waste. Economically, a significant number of developing countries depend on the extraction of rare earth minerals for smartphone production, often under exploitative conditions and poor labor standards. Socially and psychologically, smartphones have adverse effects on attention spans, social skills, and mental health. For example, some studies show attention spans are reduced due to smartphone use, and that constant conflict with information, memory, and social connection can be overwhelming. These effects can also create internal problems that affect the well-being of the individual, household, and wider society.

Daily usage of phones

  • Communication: Phones are used to make calls and sending text messages to others.
  • Social Media: Platforms like Instagram, TikTok, Facebook, Twitter
  • Navigation: Using GPS for directions and location tracking, and using built-in tools like the clock, calculator, or weather app.
  • Productivity: Apps like Calendar, setting alarms, reminders and timers, and managing tasks
